03 · Research Interests

What I'm working toward

The question that keeps surfacing in my work: why do autonomous systems that perform well in structured environments degrade predictably when conditions change? At Strider, benchmarking multiple odometry and mapping stacks across multi-floor facilities and outdoor environments has shown that state estimation accuracy alone doesn't determine field performance — the gap between controlled-setting evaluation and real deployment is systematic, not random.

I want to work on making state estimation and scene understanding more adaptable — combining principled probabilistic methods with learned representations in ways that generalise beyond training environments. The intersection of classical SLAM and learning-based approaches is, I think, where the real-world reliability problem has to be addressed.
